## PART 2: SERVICE PROCEDURES

### 2.1 Express Detail (45 min)

**Step 1: Initial Assessment (5 min)**
- Walk around vehicle
- Note any damage (document with photo)
- Check interior condition
- Confirm service package

**Step 2: Pre-Wash (5 min)**
- Rinse vehicle
- Snow foam (if applicable)
- Bug/tar removal

**Step 3: Wash & Dry (15 min)**
- Two-bucket method
- Clean wheels/tires first
- Microfiber drying
- Door jambs, gas cap

**Step 4: Interior Express (15 min)**
- Vacuum seats, floor
- Wipe dash, console
- Clean windows
- Air freshener

**Step 5: Final Walkthrough (5 min)**
- Check all areas
- Touch up if needed
- Customer sign-off

### 2.2 Signature Detail (1.5 hrs)

**Includes Express +:**

**Step 6: Deep Clean Interior (20 min)**
- Full vacuum including trunk
- Detail brushes for crevices
- Clean all surfaces
- Leather conditioning
- Steam clean (if applicable)

**Step 7: Exterior (15 min)**
- Clay bar treatment
- Engine cleaning (optional)
- Wheel well detail
- Tire dressing (non-sling)

### 2.3 Premium Detail (3+ hrs)

**Includes Signature +:**

**Step 8: Paint Correction (30-60 min)**
- Assess paint condition
- Compound if needed
- Polish
- IPA wipe down

**Step 9: Protection (15 min)**
- Apply sealant/wax
- Or ceramic coating prep

## PART 3: QUALITY CONTROL

### 3.1 Quality Checklist

**Before Customer Inspection:**
- [ ] No water spots
- [ ] No streaks on glass
- [ ] No residue on paint
- [ ] Wheels spotless
- [ ] Tires dressed
- [ ] Interior smells fresh
- [ ] No dust left behind
- [ ] Floor mats back in place

### 3.2 Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|
| Water spots | Dry completely, use filtered water |
| Streaks on glass | Use proper glass cleaner, microfiber |
| Swirl marks | Use clean pads, proper technique |
| Missed spots | Follow checklist |
| Bad smell | Use proper cleaners, air out vehicle |

### 3.3 If Customer is Unhappy

1. Listen to complaint
2. Acknowledge concern
3. Offer to fix immediately
4. If can't fix, offer to redo or refund
5. Document incident
6. Learn from feedback

## PART 10: SCALING OPERATIONS

### 10.1 When to Hire

**Signs you're ready:**
- Turning down work
- Can't keep up with schedule
- Working 60+ hours/week
- Revenue supports employee pay

### 10.2 First Hire: What Position?

**Option A: Detail Helper**
- Assists with jobs
- No experience needed
- Train them yourself
- Pay: $15-20/hour

**Option B: Another Detailer**
- Has own equipment
- Can work independently
- Split revenue (e.g., 60/40)
- Pay: 60% of job price
